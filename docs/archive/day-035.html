事件管理與災難復原

Incident Management & DR
01 — 事件管理與災難復原

Domain 7 收尾：事件回應生命週期與災難復原（DR）。當預防失敗，能否快速偵測、回應、復原決定損失大小。這也呼應 Domain 1 的 BCP。

🗺️ 本週回顧：①調查鑑識 → ②日誌/SIEM → ③組態/變更/修補 → ④維運/特權 → ⑤今天：事件管理與 DR。下週進入 Domain 8（軟體開發安全）。
02 — 事件回應生命週期
準備→偵測/分析→遏制→根除→復原→經驗學習

Containment（遏制）先止血、避免擴散；Eradication（根除）清除病灶；Recovery（復原）回到正常並監看；最後Lessons Learned 回饋改善。

03 — 災難復原與備援站點
站點就緒度成本/復原速度
Hot site 熱站設備+資料即時就緒最貴/最快
Warm site 溫站設備就緒、資料需載入中/中
Cold site 冷站僅空間與基礎設施最便宜/最慢

💡 RTO（多快救回）與 RPO（可容忍丟多少資料）決定該選哪種站點與備份頻率（細節 D7/D1 已鋪陳）。

04 — 關鍵名詞速查
Incident Response（事件回應）— 準備→偵測→遏制→根除→復原→經驗的循環。
Containment（遏制）— 限制事件擴散、止血。
RTO（復原時間目標）— 可容忍的最長中斷時間。
RPO（復原點目標）— 可容忍的最大資料遺失量。
Hot/Warm/Cold Site（備援站點）— 就緒度與成本/速度的取捨。
3-2-1 Backup（備份原則）— 3 份、2 種媒體、1 份異地（離線）。
05 — 易混淆釐清

RTO vs RPO：RTO＝『多快要救回服務』（時間）；RPO＝『可接受丟多少資料』（資料量/備份頻率）。兩者驅動 DR 設計。

遏制 vs 根除：先遏制（止血、隔離）再根除（清除病灶）。順序錯（先重建未根除）會再次淪陷。

06 — 條文與標準

NIST SP 800-61（事件處理指南）定義生命週期；SP 800-34（應變計畫）與 ISO 22301（營運持續）規範 DR/BC。勒索防禦核心是離線/不可變備份＋還原演練（呼應 Domain 6）。

07 — 真實事件：Colonial Pipeline 2021
🔎 一條輸油管，半個美東加油站缺油

2021 年美國最大燃油管線 Colonial Pipeline 遭 DarkSide 勒索軟體攻擊，公司主動停運以遏制擴散，造成東岸大規模燃油短缺，最終支付數百萬美元贖金（部分後被追回）。入侵起點是一個未啟用 MFA 的 VPN 帳號（呼應 Domain 5）。

教訓：事件回應的遏制決策（停運止血）影響巨大；預防面的 MFA、修補、備份缺一不可。可用性中斷可升級為全國級危機。

08 — CISSP 考點提醒

高頻考點：事件回應步驟順序（準備→偵測→遏制→根除→復原→經驗）；『先止血』＝Containment。RTO/RPO 與站點選擇。

常見陷阱：遏制要先於根除/復原；備份要離線且測過還原才擋得住勒索。

✏️ 一句話總結 + 自我檢測（點開看解答）

一句話：事件回應：準備→偵測→遏制→根除→復原→經驗；DR 用 RTO/RPO 決定熱/溫/冷站與備份；勒索靠離線備份＋還原演練。

Q1 . 事件回應中『先止血、防擴散』是哪一步？
→ Containment 遏制（在根除與復原之前）。

Q2 . RTO 與 RPO 分別衡量什麼？
→ RTO＝可容忍中斷時間；RPO＝可容忍資料遺失量。

Q3 . Colonial Pipeline 2021 的入侵起點？
→ 一個未啟用 MFA 的 VPN 帳號（勒索軟體）。

🎯 實戰演練 EXTRA
QUESTION 1

A financial firm's business impact analysis sets an RTO of under one hour for its core trading system. Which alternate site strategy BEST meets this requirement?

（金融公司的營運衝擊分析（BIA）要求核心交易系統的 RTO 低於 1 小時。哪種備援站點策略最能滿足需求?）

A. A warm site with equipment ready but data still to be loaded 溫站（設備就緒、資料待載入）
B. A hot site with fully configured systems and near-real-time data replication 熱站（系統就緒、資料近即時複寫）
C. A cold site with power, HVAC, and floor space only 冷站（僅供電、空調與空間）
D. Offsite backup tapes with systems rebuilt on demand 異地磁帶備份,災時再重建系統
✅ 解答與解析

正解：B。RTO 低於 1 小時代表幾乎不容中斷,只有熱站（設備＋資料即時就緒）能在此時間內接手。判斷邏輯:由 BIA 的 RTO/RPO 反推站點等級——目標越嚴,站點越熱、成本越高;成本由風險需求正當化,而非一味省錢。

為何其他選項錯：
  • A：溫站需載入資料與啟動服務,復原通常以『小時到天』計,難以保證 1 小時內。
  • C：冷站只有場地與基礎設施,復原以『天到週』計,完全不符。
  • D：磁帶還原加重建系統最慢,連溫站都不如。
QUESTION 2

A company keeps its only two backup copies on a NAS located in the same data center as production. Ransomware encrypts the production data and both backup copies. Which practice would have BEST prevented this total data loss?

（某公司僅有的兩份備份都放在與正式環境同一機房的 NAS 上;勒索軟體把正式資料與兩份備份全數加密。哪個做法最能避免這種全損?）

A. Increasing the backup frequency to hourly 將備份頻率提高到每小時
B. Encrypting the backup copies 將備份加密
C. Following the 3-2-1 rule with at least one offline, offsite copy 遵循 3-2-1 原則,至少一份離線異地備份
D. Failing over to a warm site 切換到溫站
✅ 解答與解析

正解：C。兩份備份與正式環境同機房、同網路,攻擊面重疊,勒索軟體一次通殺。3-2-1 原則（3 份備份、2 種媒體、1 份異地且離線/不可變）確保至少一份備份不在攻擊面內,是勒索防禦的核心;再搭配定期還原演練驗證備份可用。

為何其他選項錯：
  • A：頻率再高,備份仍在同一攻擊面內,一樣會被加密,只是丟得比較新。
  • B：加密保護的是機密性,擋不住備份被『再加密/破壞』的可用性攻擊。
  • D：溫站復原仍需乾淨的備份資料可載入;備份全毀時,切換站點也無資料可還原。
📑 官方考綱對照重點（Official Objectives · §7.6 事件管理 / §7.10 復原）
▍事件回應步驟（DRMRRRL）

Preparation → Detection 偵測 → Response 回應 → Mitigation 緩解(containment 控制) → Reporting 報告 → Recovery 復原 → Remediation 矯正(根因) → Lessons Learned 教訓。緩解＝控制不是修好；不得反擊攻擊者。

▍復原指標

RPO（可容忍資料遺失）／RTO（可容忍復原時間）／WRT（驗證整合時間）／MTD（最大可容忍中斷）。站點：hot/warm/cold。

對應官方 §7.6 事件管理、§7.10 復原策略。

§7.6.1 Detection
• Detection: the identification of potential security incidents via monitoring and analyzing security logs, threat intelligence, or incident reports; as above, understanding the distinction between an event and an incident, the goal of detection is to identify an adverse event (an incident) and begin dealing with it
• Common methods to detect incidents:
• intrusion detection and prevention systems
• antimalware
• automated tools that scan audit logs looking for predefined events
• end users sometimes detect irregular activity and contact support
• Note: receiving an alert or complaint doesn’t always mean an incident has occurred
§7.10.2 Recovery site strategies (e.g., cold vs. hot, resource capacity agreements)
• Non-disaster: service disruption with significant but limited impact
• Disaster: event that causes an entire site to be unusable for a day or longer (usually requires alternate processing facility)
• Natural disaster: events that commonly threaten orgs including earthquakes, floods, storms, fires, tsunamis, and volcanic eruptions
• Human-caused disasters: explosions, electrical fires, terrorist acts, power outages and other utility failures, hardware/software failures, labor difficulties, theft, vandalism, etc.
• Catastrophe: major disruption that destroys the facility altogether
• For disasters and catastrophes, an org has 3 basic options:
• use a dedicated site that the org owns/operates
• lease a commercial facility (hot, warm, cold site)
• enter into a formal agreement with another facility/org
• When a disaster interrupts a business, a disaster recovery plan should kick in nearly automatically and begin providing support for recovery operations
• in addition to improving your response capabilities, purchasing insurance can reduce the impact of financial losses
• Recovery site strategies consider multiple elements of an organization, such as people, data, infrastructure, and cost, as well as factors like availability and location
• When designing a disaster recovery plan, it’s important to keep your goal in mind — the restoration of workgroups to the point that they can resume their activities in their usual work locations
• sometimes it's best to develop separate recovery facilities for different work groups
• To recover your business operations with the greatest possible efficiency, you should engineer the disaster recovery plan so that those business units with the highest priority are recovered first
• Mutual Assistance Agreements (MAA): provide an inexpensive alternative to disaster recovery sites; *not commonly used* because they are difficult to enforce; orgs participating in an MAA may also be shut down by the same disaster, and MAAs raise confidentiality concerns
• Resource Capacity Agreements: pre-arranged vendor agreements to secure the necessary resources required after a disruptive event; the goal is to ensure an org has access to resources at a recovery site
