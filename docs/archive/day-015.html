實體與場所安全

Physical & Site Security
01 — 別忘了實體世界

再強的加密也擋不住「有人把伺服器搬走」或「機房淹水斷電」。實體與場所安全（Physical Security）是 Domain 3 的收尾，也是常被技術人輕忽的考點。今天看場所設計（CPTED）、電力、環境/空調、滅火、機房分級。

🗺️ 本週回顧：①設計原則 → ②安全模型 → ③密碼學 → ④系統/雲端弱點 → ⑤今天：實體與場所安全。下週進入 Domain 4（通訊與網路安全）。
02 — CPTED 與分層實體防護

CPTED（Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design，以環境設計預防犯罪）透過三概念降低犯罪：自然監視（視線開闊）、自然存取控制（動線引導）、領域感（明確界線）。實體防護同樣採縱深：圍籬 → 門禁 → 警衛/CCTV → 上鎖機櫃層層套疊。

03 — 電力與環境
電力：UPS 撐短時間、發電機撐長時間；認識 brownout（持續低電壓）/ blackout（停電）/ surge（突波）
環境/空調（HVAC）：控溫控濕；機房常用正壓防塵、冷熱通道（hot/cold aisle）配置散熱
偵測：煙霧/溫度/漏水感測，及早告警
04 — 滅火系統
類型適用/重點
Wet pipe 濕管管內常存水，最常見；但機房漏水風險
Dry pipe / Pre-action 乾管/預動平時無水，降低誤觸漏水，適合機房
Gas（如 FM-200） 氣體不傷設備、適合機房；須注意人員氧氣
Class 分級電器火災屬 Class C（美），須用不導電滅火劑

💡 機房滅火優先考量「不傷設備＋人員安全」：常用氣體系統或預動式；電器火災不可用水或一般泡沫。

05 — 關鍵名詞速查
CPTED（環境設計預防犯罪）— 自然監視/存取控制/領域感降低犯罪。
UPS（不斷電系統）— 停電時短時供電，配合發電機。
Brownout（持續低電壓）— 電壓不足，可能損壞設備。
Hot/Cold Aisle（冷熱通道）— 機房散熱配置，提升冷卻效率。
Mantrap / Access Control Vestibule（雙門互鎖）— 一次一人通過，防尾隨（tailgating）。
Fire Suppression（滅火系統）— 濕管/乾管/氣體；機房重視不傷設備與人安。
06 — 易混淆釐清

Fail-safe vs Fail-secure（門禁）：火災等人命情境，門應 fail-safe（斷電即開讓人逃生）；高安全資產區可能要 fail-secure（斷電即鎖）。看『保命 vs 保資產』取捨。

Blackout vs Brownout：blackout＝完全停電；brownout＝持續電壓不足。兩者都靠 UPS/發電機緩解。

07 — 條文與標準

機房可參考 Uptime Institute Tier I–IV 分級（冗餘度遞增）；NIST SP 800-53 的 PE（Physical & Environmental）控制族涵蓋實體與環境要求；消防分級參考 NFPA。

08 — 真實事件：機房火災/斷電
🔎 雲端也怕火與電

2021 年某大型歐洲資料中心發生嚴重火災，導致大量客戶網站與資料中斷，部分未異地備份的客戶永久失去資料——再次證明可用性（A）與實體/環境控制是真實風險，不是紙上談兵。電力與冷卻一旦失效，整個機房可在數分鐘內停擺。

教訓：實體安全＝CIA 的可用性地基；異地備份、冗餘電力/冷卻、滅火設計缺一不可。呼應 Domain 1 的 BCP/DR。

09 — CISSP 考點提醒

選最佳答案：「防尾隨進入機房」→ mantrap/雙門互鎖；「機房滅火不傷設備」→ 氣體系統；「停電維持運作」→ UPS＋發電機。

常見陷阱：涉及人命時門禁應 fail-safe（開門逃生）而非鎖死；電器火災（Class C）不可用水。實體控制常是『最佳答案』裡被忽略的那個。

✏️ 一句話總結 + 自我檢測（點開看解答）

一句話：實體安全守 CIA 的可用性：CPTED 設計、分層門禁、UPS/發電機、HVAC 與冷熱通道、機房用氣體/預動滅火，並異地備份。

Q1 . 防尾隨（tailgating）最有效的實體控制？
→ Mantrap／雙門互鎖（access control vestibule）。

Q2 . 機房滅火為何偏好氣體系統？
→ 不傷電子設備、滅火快；但須注意人員氧氣。

Q3 . 火災時資料中心大門該 fail-safe 還 fail-secure？
→ Fail-safe（斷電開門）以保人命逃生。

🎯 實戰演練 EXTRA
QUESTION 1

In CPTED, which of the following BEST illustrates the concept of natural surveillance?

（在 CPTED 中,下列何者最能說明「自然監視（natural surveillance）」的概念?）

A. Designing open sightlines and good lighting so activity around the facility is easily observed 設計開闊視線與良好照明,使場所周遭活動容易被看見
B. Installing CCTV cameras monitored by security guards 安裝由警衛監看的 CCTV 攝影機
C. Using low fences and signage to mark the property boundary 以矮圍籬與標示界定場所範圍
D. Landscaping that guides all visitors toward a single controlled entrance 以景觀動線引導訪客走向單一管制入口
✅ 解答與解析

正解：A。Natural surveillance＝靠「環境設計本身」（視線、照明、窗戶配置）讓正常使用者自然看得見異常,提高犯罪被發現的風險。判斷關鍵是 natural（設計使然）,不是靠技術設備或人力。

為何其他選項錯：
  • B：CCTV＋警衛屬技術/人員監視控制,不是『自然』監視。
  • C：界線與標示屬 territoriality（領域感）。
  • D：動線引導屬 natural access control（自然存取控制）。
QUESTION 2

During a heat wave, servers in a facility keep rebooting because of prolonged periods of reduced voltage. What is this power condition, and which control BEST keeps systems running?

（熱浪期間,某設施的伺服器因長時間電壓不足而反覆重開機。這是哪種電力狀況?哪個控制最能維持系統運作?）

A. Sag (momentary low voltage); add surge protectors 瞬時低壓,加裝突波保護器
B. Blackout (complete power loss); redesign hot/cold aisles 完全停電,重新設計冷熱通道
C. Brownout (prolonged low voltage); deploy a UPS backed by a generator 持續低壓,部署 UPS 並以發電機支援
D. Surge (prolonged high voltage); install a power conditioner 持續高壓,安裝電源調節器
✅ 解答與解析

正解：C。『長時間』電壓不足＝brownout（sag 是瞬時低壓）。UPS 先撐住短時間並穩壓,發電機接手長時間供電,是維持運作的正確組合。記憶:瞬時＝fault/sag/spike,持續＝blackout/brownout/surge。

為何其他選項錯：
  • A：sag 是『瞬時』低壓,與題目的『長時間』不符;突波保護器針對高壓事件。
  • B：blackout 是完全斷電;冷熱通道是散熱設計,與電力供應無關。
  • D：surge 是持續『高』壓,方向相反;電源調節器也非對付長期低壓的最佳解。
📑 官方考綱對照重點（Official Objectives · §3.9 場所與設施安全）
▍電力問題（必記）

瞬時：Fault（瞬斷）／Sag/Dip（瞬時低壓）／Spike（瞬時高壓）；持續：Blackout（停電）／Brownout（持續低壓）／Surge（持續高壓）。防護升序：突波保護器→電源調節器→UPS→發電機。

▍火災

滅火器分類：A 一般／B 液體／C 電器／D 金屬／K 油脂。抑制：wet pipe（常水）/dry pipe（壓縮氣體）/pre-action（兩段）/deluge（大量水）。保護人命永遠第一。

§3.9.6 Utilities and heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC)
• Power management in ascending order: surge protectors, power/power-line conditioner, uninterruptible power supply (UPS), generators
• Types of UPS:
• double conversion: functions by taking power from the wall outlet, storing it in a battery, pulling power out of the battery and feeding that power to the device/devices
• line-interactive: has a surge protector, battery charger/inverter and voltage regulator positioned between the grid power source and the equipment (battery is not in line under normal conditions)
• Commercial power problem types:
• fault: momentary loss of power
• blackout: usually sustained and complete loss of power
• sag: (AKA dip) momentary low voltage
• brownout: prolonged low voltage
• spike: momentary high voltage
• surge: short-duration high voltage
• inrush: initial surge of power associated with connecting to a power source
• Think through types of physical controls for HVAC:
• restrict duct space continuity to controlled areas
• use separate and redundant HVAC systems for computer equipment
• rooms containing primarily computers should have:
• temps kept at 59 to 89.6 deg Fahrenheit (15 to 32 deg Celsius)
• humidity should be maintained between 20 and 80 percent
• note ASHRAE (American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers) provides guidelines, which are the industry standard for data center environmental conditions
• note that even on nonstatic carpeting, if the env has low humidity, a 20k-volt static discharge is possible; and even minimal levels of static electricity can destroy electronic equipment
• Datacenter:
• should be on different power circuits from occupied areas
• common to use a backup generator
§3.9.8 Fire prevention, detection, and suppression
• Protecting personnel from harm should always be the most important goal of any security or protection system!
• In addition to protecting people, fire detection and suppression is designed to keep asset damage caused by fire, smoke, heat, and suppression materials to a minimum
• Fire triangle: three triangle corners represent fuel, heat, and oxygen; the center of the triangle represents the chemical reaction among these three elements
• if you can remove any one of the three items from the fire triangle, the fire can be extinguished
• Fire suppression mediums:
• water suppresses temperature
• soda acid and other dry powders suppress the fuel supply
• carbon dioxide (CO2) suppresses the oxygen supply
• halon substitutes and other nonflammable gases interfere with the chemistry of combustion and/or suppress the oxygen supply
• Fire stages:
• Stage 1: incipient stage: at this stage, there is only air ionization and no smoke
• Stage 2: smoke stage: smoke is visible from the point of ignition
• Stage 3: flame stage: this is when a flame can be seen with the naked eye
• Stage 4: heat stage: at stage 4, there is an intense heat buildup and everything in the area burns
• Fire extinguisher classes:
• Class A: common combustibles
• Class B: liquids
• Class C: electrical
• Class D: metal
• Class K: cooking material (oil/grease)
• Four main types of suppression:
• wet pipe system: (AKA closed head system): is always filled with water; water discharges immediately when suppression is triggered
• dry pipe system: contains compressed inert gas
• pre-action system: a variation of the dry pipe system that uses a two-stage detection and release mechanism
• deluge system: uses larger pipes and delivers larger volume of water
• Note: Most sprinkler heads feature a glass bulb filled with a glycerin-based liquid; this liquid expands when it comes in contact with air heated to between 135 and 165 degrees; when the liquid expands, it shatters its glass confines and the sprinkler head activates
