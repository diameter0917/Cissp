事件管理與 DR 深入

Incident Mgmt & DR Deep Dive
01 — 中期 D7 收尾：事件回應與災難復原

D7 中期收尾,深入事件回應生命週期每階段的細節、RTO/RPO 與站點選擇、與 BCP/DR 測試。當預防失敗,回應與復原的成熟度決定損失大小。

🗺️ 本週回顧:①調查鑑識 → ②SIEM/TI/UEBA → ③組態/變更/修補 → ④維運/偵測 → ⑤今天:事件管理與 DR。下週進入 Domain 8 中期。
02 — 事件回應生命週期(NIST)
準備→偵測分析→遏制→根除→復原→事後檢討
Preparation:計畫、團隊(CSIRT)、工具、演練——平時做好決定戰時成敗
Containment:先止血(短期隔離)再長期遏制;保全證據(呼應鑑識)
Eradication:清除病灶(惡意碼/後門/被建帳號)、找根因
Recovery:還原並加強監控確認未復發;Lessons Learned 轉成改善
03 — 站點與復原指標
站點就緒成本/速度
Hot 熱站設備+資料即時最貴/分鐘級
Warm 溫站設備就緒、資料需載入中/小時級
Cold 冷站僅空間設施最便宜/天級
Cloud/DRaaS彈性、按需依設計,常具彈性

💡 RTO≤MTD;RPO 決定備份頻率。站點選擇是 RTO 與成本的取捨。異地、離線/不可變備份是抗勒索關鍵(線上備份會一起被加密)。

04 — BCP/DR 測試與抗勒索
DRP 五種測試類型(§7.12,風險由低到高):① Read-through/Checklist(文件審閱):分發計畫給 DR 團隊逐項審閱完整性與正確性——不碰任何系統,風險最低
② Structured Walkthrough/Tabletop(桌上推演):團隊與關鍵利害關係人聚集,依災難情境口頭推演各自的回應步驟,不動系統與營運
③ Simulation(模擬演練):依情境實際執行部分回應措施,但仍不影響正式營運
④ Parallel(平行測試):人員進駐備援站並實際啟動復原系統,但不切換——主站照常營運
⑤ Full-interruption(完全中斷):真正關閉主站、切換到備援站——最能證明計畫有效,但風險最高、須管理層核准
抗勒索:3-2-1 備份＋離線/不可變(immutable)副本＋定期還原演練＋分段限制擴散
事件溝通:對內、對客戶、對主管機關(法遵通報時限)、對媒體——預先備腳本
決策權責:誰能決定停機/付贖/通報——事前明定(避免臨場混亂)
05 — 關鍵名詞速查
CSIRT（資安事件回應團隊）— 負責事件回應的編組。
Containment（遏制）— 限制事件擴散、止血。
Eradication（根除）— 清除病灶與根因。
RTO/RPO（復原時間/復原點）— 多快救回/可丟多少資料。
Hot/Warm/Cold Site（備援站點）— 就緒度與成本取捨。
Immutable Backup（不可變備份）— 寫入後不可改/刪,抗勒索。
Lessons Learned（事後檢討）— 回饋改善的最後階段。
06 — 易混淆釐清

遏制 vs 根除:先遏制(隔離止血)再根除(清病灶)。順序錯(未根除就復原)會再次淪陷。

RTO vs RPO:RTO『多快救回服務』(時間);RPO『可丟多少資料』(備份頻率)。

BCP vs DRP:BCP 整個業務持續(人/流程/溝通);DRP 是其中 IT 復原子集。

線上備份的陷阱:勒索會加密連網的備份;必須有離線/不可變副本且實測還原。

07 — 條文與標準

NIST SP 800-61(事件處理)/800-34(應變計畫)/800-84(測試)、ISO 22301(BC)/27035(事件管理)。法遵通報時限(如 GDPR 72 小時)影響回應流程設計(D1)。

08 — 真實事件：Maersk / NotPetya 2017
🔎 十分鐘癱瘓全球航運

2017 NotPetya(偽裝勒索的破壞性 wiper,經烏克蘭會計軟體更新供應鏈散布)在數十分鐘內癱瘓航運巨頭 Maersk 約 4,000 台伺服器、4.5 萬台 PC,全球貨運停擺,損失約 3 億美元。Maersk 幾乎所有網域控制器被毀,靠一台在迦納因斷電而離線、倖存的 DC 備份才得以重建——近乎奇蹟。

教訓:離線/異地備份是最後防線(線上的都被毀);供應鏈攻擊(D1)可瞬間全域擴散,分段限制爆炸半徑至關重要;DR 計畫必須假設『核心基礎設施全毀』並演練重建。

📌 延伸：勒索事件的決策困境

勒索事件常面臨付不付贖的兩難:付款不保證能還原、助長犯罪、且可能違反制裁法規(付給受制裁實體違法);不付則面臨資料公開與停機。正解是事前避免被逼到此處——可靠的離線備份＋還原演練讓組織有『不付款也能復原』的底氣。決策權責、法務、保險、執法聯繫都應事前規劃。

09 — CISSP 考點提醒

必考:事件回應順序(準備→偵測→遏制→根除→復原→經驗);先遏制再根除;RTO≤MTD;抗勒索靠離線/不可變備份＋還原演練。

選最佳答案:『先止血防擴散』→ containment;『選備援站』→ 依 RTO/成本;『核心全毀仍能復原』→ 異地離線備份＋分段。

✏️ 一句話總結 + 自我檢測（點開看解答）

一句話：事件回應:準備→偵測→遏制→根除→復原→經驗(先遏制再根除);RTO≤MTD、RPO 定備份頻率、熱溫冷站;抗勒索靠離線/不可變備份＋演練;NotPetya 示警供應鏈與分段。

Q1 . 事件回應為何要先遏制再根除？
→ 先止血防擴散,再清除病灶;順序錯(未根除就復原)會再次淪陷。

Q2 . 為何線上備份不足以抗勒索？
→ 勒索會加密連網備份;需離線/不可變副本且實測還原。

Q3 . NotPetya 中 Maersk 靠什麼重建？
→ 一台因斷電離線而倖存的網域控制器備份(凸顯離線/異地備份的關鍵)。

🎯 實戰演練 EXTRA
QUESTION 1

During an annual DR exercise, the recovery team travels to the alternate site and fully activates the recovery systems using restored data, while the primary data center continues serving production without interruption. Which type of DRP test is this?

（年度 DR 演練中,復原團隊進駐備援站,以還原的資料實際啟動復原系統;主資料中心則持續正常服務、未受中斷。這是哪一種 DRP 測試?）

A. Simulation test 模擬演練
B. Parallel test 平行測試
C. Full-interruption test 完全中斷測試
D. Structured walkthrough 桌上推演
✅ 解答與解析

正解：B。判斷邏輯:鑑別點有二——①備援站實際啟動(排除只口頭推演或只做部分措施);②主站照常營運、不切換。兩者同時成立正是 parallel test 的定義;若真把主站關閉並切換,才升級為 full-interruption。五種測試風險由低到高:read-through→walkthrough→simulation→parallel→full-interruption。

為何其他選項錯：
  • A：simulation 只依情境『實際執行部分』回應措施,不會完整啟動備援站系統。
  • C：full-interruption 必須真正關閉主站並切換;本題主站未中斷。
  • D：structured walkthrough 是團隊聚集口頭推演各自步驟,不動任何系統。
QUESTION 2

A BIA concludes that a trading platform has a maximum tolerable downtime of two hours and can lose almost no transaction data. Which recovery strategy BEST meets these requirements?

（BIA 認定某交易平台的最大可容忍中斷(MTD)為兩小時,且幾乎不能遺失交易資料。哪種復原策略最符合需求?）

A. A warm site with equipment in place, restored from nightly backups 溫站(設備就緒,以每晚備份還原資料)
B. A hot site kept synchronized through near-real-time data replication 熱站(以近即時資料抄寫保持同步)
C. A cold site with facility space ready for equipment delivery 冷站(僅備妥場地設施,待設備進駐)
D. A reciprocal agreement with a partner organization's data center 與夥伴組織資料中心簽訂互惠協議
✅ 解答與解析

正解：B。判斷邏輯:兩個需求各卡一關——MTD 兩小時代表 RTO 必須≤2 小時,只有分鐘級就緒的熱站辦得到;『幾乎不能掉資料』代表 RPO 趨近零,需要近即時抄寫。站點選擇是 RTO/RPO 與成本的取捨:需求最嚴,就得付最貴的熱站。

為何其他選項錯：
  • A：溫站復原以小時計已屬勉強,且每晚備份代表最壞可掉近 24 小時資料,違反 RPO 要求。
  • C：冷站復原以天計,遠遠超出 2 小時的 MTD。
  • D：互惠協議的可用性與環境相容性無保證,無法承諾 2 小時 RTO 與近零 RPO。
QUESTION 3

A business unit states it can tolerate losing at most one hour of order data, yet the only protection for the order database is a nightly backup at 23:00. Which metric is NOT being met, and what is the BEST corrective action?

（業務單位表明訂單資料最多只能遺失一小時,但訂單資料庫目前唯一的保護是每晚 23:00 的備份。哪個指標未被滿足?最佳矯正措施為何?）

A. RTO is not met; acquire a hot site to speed up recovery RTO 未達標;建置熱站加速復原
B. MTD is not met; negotiate a longer tolerable outage with the business MTD 未達標;與業務協商延長可容忍中斷時間
C. RPO is not met; increase backup frequency or adopt continuous replication RPO 未達標;提高備份頻率或改採連續抄寫
D. MTTR is not met; assign more staff to the recovery team MTTR 未達標;為復原團隊增派人力
✅ 解答與解析

正解：C。判斷邏輯:『可遺失多少資料』對應的是 RPO(recovery point objective)——每晚備份的最壞情況會掉近 24 小時資料,遠超 1 小時容忍度。RPO 由備份/抄寫頻率決定,矯正就是把備份間隔壓到 1 小時內或改用連續抄寫。記憶法:RPO=Point=可掉多少資料;RTO=Time=多快救回服務。

為何其他選項錯：
  • A：RTO 關乎『多快恢復服務』;題目談的是資料遺失量,熱站救不了備份間隔太疏的問題。
  • B：MTD 是可容忍的停機時間上限,與資料遺失量無關;要業務遷就 IT 也本末倒置。
  • D：MTTR 是平均修復時間指標;增派人力不會改變備份頻率造成的資料缺口。
📑 官方考綱對照重點（Official Objectives · §7.6 事件 / §7.10 復原（深入））
▍備份類型與還原

Full 完整（全部，還原最快）／Incremental 增量（自上次任何備份的變更，重設 archive bit，還原需 full＋全部增量）／Differential 差異（自上次 full 的變更，不動 archive bit，還原只需 full＋最後差異）。3-2-1 原則。

▍RAID 與站點

RAID 0 條帶(速)／1 鏡像(備)／5 單同位(≥3 碟)／6 雙同位(≥4 碟,可壞2)／10 鏡像+條帶。站點成本/時間：cold < warm < hot < redundant。

§7.10.1 Backup storage strategies (e.g., cloud storage, onsite, offsite)
• Backup strategies are driven by org goals and objectives and usually focus on backup and restore time as well as storage needs; the goal is to determine how and where data is stored for recovery in case of data loss, corruption, or disaster
• 3-2-1 rule:
• 3 copies of critical files
• 2 backups on different media
• 1 backup stored offsite
• Onsite backup: storing backup data within the same location as the source data (AKA local backup); onsite backups have the downside that if through some disaster you lose your source data, there is a chance you could also lose your backup
• Offsite backup: storing backup data at a different location from where the original data is stored; the offsite location should be geographically remote from, or far enough away from the source data that a single disaster doesn't compromise both
• Cloud backup: storing backup data in the cloud, with the advantages of storage scalability, high availability, and you pay only for what you use
• Archive bit: technical detail (metadata) that indicates the status of a backup relative to a given backup strategy
• 0 = file has been backed up (no new changes)
• 1 = file has been modified since last backup (needs to be backed up)
• Different backup strategies deal with the archive bit differently; Incremental and differential backup strategies don't treat the archive bit in the same manner
• once a full backup is complete, the archive bit on every file is reset, turned off, or set to 0
• Three types of backups:
• Full backup: store a complete copy of the data contained on the protected device or backup media; full backups duplicate every file on the system regardless of the setting of the archive bit
• Incremental backup: backs up only data that has changed since the last backup (whether full or incremental)
• only files that have the archive bit turned on, enabled, or set to 1 are duplicated
• once an incremental backup is complete, the archive bit on all duplicated files is reset, turned off, or set to 0
• Differential backup: changes since the last full backup
• only files that have the archive bit turned on, enabled, or set to 1 are duplicated
• unlike full and incremental backups, the differential backup process does not change the archive bit
• the most important difference between incremental and differential backups is the time needed to restore data in the event of an emergency
• a combination of full and differential backups will require only two backups to be restored: the most recent full backup and the most recent differential backup
• a combination of full backups with incremental backups will require restoration of the most recent full backups as well as all incremental backups performed since that full backup
• differential backups don’t take as long to restore, but they take longer to create than incremental
• Note: Grandfather/Father/Son, Tower of Hanoi, and Six Cartridge Weekly are all different approaches to rotating backup media, balancing media reuse with data retention concerns
• Grandfather/Father/Son (GFS): three or more backup cycles, such as daily, weekly and monthly; the daily backups are rotated on a 3-months basis using a FIFO system, the weekly backups are similarly rotated on a bi-yearly basis, and the monthly backup on a yearly basis
• Tower of Hanoi: based on the puzzle of the same name, where the first backup is overwritten every other day, the second backup is overwritten every fourth day, and the third backup is overwritten every other day at a different increment than the first backup
• Six Cartridge Weekly: a method that involves six different media (cartridge, tape, drives etc) used for each day of the week; many small businesses that do not need to backup high volumes of data use this type of tape rotation schedule, and usually consists of using four media for incremental and differential backups between Monday and Thursday
• Backup storage best practices include keeping copies of the media in at least one offsite location to provide redundancy should the primary location be unavailable, incapacitated, or destroyed; common strategy is to store backups in a cloud service that is itself geographically redundant
• Two common backup strategies:
• if a failure occurs Saturday morning, restore Monday’s full backup and then restore only Friday’s differential backup
• if a failure occurs Saturday morning, restore Monday’s full backup and then restore each incremental backup in the original chronological order
• Three main techniques used to create offsite copies of database content: electronic vaulting, remote journaling, and remote mirroring
• electronic vaulting: where database backups are moved to a remote site using bulk transfers
• remote journaling: data transfers are performed in a more expeditious manner; remote journaling is similar to electronic vaulting in that transaction logs transferred to the remote site are not applied to a live database server but are maintained in a backup device
• remote mirroring: the most advanced db backup solution, and the most expensive, with remote mirroring, a live db server is maintained at the backup site; the remote server receives copies of the db modifications at the same time they are applied to the production server at the primary site
§7.10.4 System resilience, High Availability (HA), Quality of Service (QoS), and fault tolerance
• System resilience: the ability of a system to maintain an acceptable level of service during an adverse event
• High Availability (HA): the use of redundant technology components to allow a system to quickly recover from a failure after experiencing a brief disruption
• Clustering: refers to a group of systems working together to handle workloads; often seen in the context of web servers that use a load balancer to manage incoming traffic, and distributes requests to multiple web servers (the cluster)
• Redundancy: unlike a cluster, where all members work together, redundancy typically involves a primary and secondary system; the primary system does all the work, and the secondary system is in standby mode unless the primary system fails, at which time activity can fail over to the secondary
• Both clustering and redundancy include high availability as a by-product of their configuration
• Quality of Service (QoS): controls protect the availability of data networks under load
• many factors contribute to the quality of the end-user experience and QoS attempts to manage all of these factors to create an experience that meets business requirements
• factors contributing to QoS:
• bandwidth: the network capacity available to carry communications
• latency: the time it takes a packet to travel from source to destination
• packet loss: some packets may be lost between source and destination, requiring re-transmission
• interference: electrical noise, faulty equipment, and other factors may corrupt the contents of packets
• Fault tolerance: the ability of a system to suffer a fault but continue to operate
• Redundant array of independent disks (RAID): refers to multiple drives being used in unison in a system to achieve greater speed or availability; the most well-known RAID levels are:
• RAID 0—Striping: provides significant speed, writing and reading advantages
• RAID 1—Mirroring: uses redundancy to provide reliable availability of data
• RAID 5—Parity Protection: requires a minimum of three drives and provides a cost-effective balance between RAID 0 and RAID 1; RAID 5 utilizes a parity bit, computed from an XOR operation, for purposes of storing and restoring data
• RAID 6—Double Parity: similar to RAID 5 but uses two parity blocks, allowing two drives to fail simultaneously without data loss; requires a minimum of four drives
• RAID 10—Mirroring and Striping: requires a minimum of four drives and provides the benefits of striping (speed) and mirroring (availability) in one solution; this type of RAID is typically one of the most expensive
